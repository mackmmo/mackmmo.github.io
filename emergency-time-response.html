Emergency response time is defined by the amount of time it takes to travel by fire engine or ambulance, from a fire station, to a specified address. Being able to quantify a location’s response time has implications for construction of new hospitals or other emergency response facilities, insurance policies, etc. The data I have created in this project could potentially impact future lives. Understanding how long it takes for an ambulance or fire crew to reach a residence could very well be a deciding factor for home location. Especially for those who have compromised health.

Tools used: ArcGIS Pro, network analysis, spline interpolation, vector analysis.
